2) How the industries are changing


The data below shows the change in employment from 2006 to 2015, and then generates an estimate for how industries might change through 2020. The dashed red line compares the local trend with the national trend. Circles above the line indicate a more positive local trend and possibly some sort of competitive advantage, while circles below the line might indicate competitive disadvantage. Economic development practitioners can talk with their industries to identify the causes for this situation. In turn, they can build upon their strengths and either improve or hedge against their weaknesses.


Use the scroll bar beneath the graph to move from year to year. When you hover over a circle, the industry name appears above the graph and the industry's trend path - how it changes from 2006 to 2020 - appears as a line.
